What is sextortion

Sextortion is a form of extortion. Extortion is defined as the use of coercion to obtain money, goods, or services from an individual.​ Which also counts as blackmail. More specifically sextortion is extortion of sexual images/content or extorsion using sexual images/content

Sextortion is a serious crime that occurs when someone threatens to distribute your private and sensitive material if you don’t provide them images of a sexual nature, sexual favors, or money.The perpetrator may also threaten to harm your friends or relatives by using information they have obtained from your electronic devices unless you comply with their demands.

Most Common Group

Teenage Boys Currently the number-one targeted group in financial sextortion cases. Offenders (often overseas criminal networks) pretend to be a girl their age and pressure them into sending explicit images. Boys are more likely to panic, hide the situation, and pay quickly—making them high-reward targets.

Young Men (18–25) Especially on dating apps, Instagram, Snapchat, and Discord. More likely to engage with strangers online and send photos impulsively. Attackers use both romantic lures and fake “underage girl” threats.

Teen Girls & Young Women Still targeted, especially in sextortion for control, relationship coercion, or harassment. Often involves ex-partners, classmates, or people they know. Cases include threats to release images, stalking, and emotional manipulation.

Adults in High-Stress or High-Trust Roles Teachers, military members, medical staff, and professionals. Offenders assume these individuals will pay to hide the situation due to reputation or career risks.

    Immediate Steps for the Victim

    Stop all communication with the extortionist Do NOT pay anything. Paying almost always leads to more demands. Block them on every platform, but do not delete the chat/profile yet (evidence).

    Preserve all evidence

    Take screenshots of: The threats Their username/profile links (OnlyFans, Snapchat, Instagram, Telegram, etc.) Any photos/videos they claim to have Payment demands (Bitcoin wallet, CashApp, etc.) Do NOT delete the accounts or conversations yet.

    Deactivate (don’t delete) social media temporarily

    Facebook/Instagram: Settings “Take a break” or temporarily deactivate. Snapchat/TikTok: Same option exists. This prevents the extortionist from easily finding friends/family.

    Report the account everywhere it appeared Platform reporting (Instagram, Snapchat, TikTok, Twitter/X, Facebook, Discord, etc.)
